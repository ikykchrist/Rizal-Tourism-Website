Tanay

Home of Adventure and Nature Experience (HANE)

Tradition

Tanay, a picturesque town in Rizal Province, is known not only for its natural wonders but also for its rich cultural and religious traditions. One of its most important and enduring traditions is the annual celebration of the Feast of San Ildefonso, the town’s patron saint. Held every January 23, this event blends deep Catholic devotion with festive Filipino spirit.


The highlight of this celebration is the “Parada ng mga Kawayan” (Parade of Bamboo), where locals decorate bamboo poles with colorful materials and fruits, symbolizing thanksgiving for a bountiful harvest. Traditional dances, music, and local delicacies are shared during the celebration, turning the town into a hub of faith and festivity.


Another key tradition in Tanay is its observance of Holy Week, particularly the “Senakulo”, a dramatic reenactment of the Passion of Christ. Local residents participate as actors in this Lenten tradition, which draws both devotees and tourists to witness the live performances in open spaces or church courtyards.


Tanay also values its indigenous roots, especially the culture of the Dumagat-Remontado communities in the Sierra Madre. Their traditions, rituals, and crafts are preserved and respected by the local government and cultural organizations.


These traditions make Tanay a unique town where faith, nature, and heritage come together in a meaningful way.

Festivals

Feast of San Ildefonso

Held every January 23, this festival honors San Ildefonso, the patron saint of Tanay. The celebration includes religious processions, street dancing, and traditional activities. It’s a major event that brings the community together for prayer, thanksgiving, and festivity.

Holy Week Observances (Senakulo)

During Holy Week, Tanay holds a dramatic reenactment of the Passion of Christ called Senakulo. The local community actively participates in the performance, which draws large crowds. It’s a solemn yet significant tradition that reflects the town's religious devotion.

Hane Festival

The Hane Festival is an annual celebration held in Tanay, Rizal, commemorating the town's founding anniversary. Typically occurring in November, the festival spans several days and features a variety of activities that highlight Tanay's rich cultural heritage, agricultural abundance, and community spirit.

Must Visit

Daranak Falls

A scenic and serene natural spot, Daranak Falls is one of the most popular destinations in Tanay. The falls cascade into a cool, inviting pool, perfect for swimming and relaxing. Surrounded by lush greenery, it's an ideal spot for nature lovers and those looking to escape the city.

Tinipak River

Tinipak River, nestled in the boundaries of Tanay, Rizal, and General Nakar, Quezon, is a serene destination celebrated for its crystal-clear waters, unique marble rock formations, and proximity to the Sierra Madre mountains.

Tanay Church (St. Ildephonsus of Toledo Parish Church)

One of the oldest churches in Rizal, the Tanay Church has significant historical and cultural value. The church, which was originally built in the 1600s, is an excellent place to appreciate Tanay's religious history and architectural beauty. It’s also the center of the Feast of San Ildefonso, one of the major celebrations in the town.

Local Foods

Lechon Kawali

A well-loved Filipino dish, Lechon Kawali is deep-fried crispy pork belly served with a side of liver sauce or soy sauce. It’s a favorite in Tanay and is often served during special occasions or fiestas.

Buko Pie

A delicious coconut pie filled with fresh, sweet coconut meat and custard. This tasty treat is widely loved in Tanay and is a must-try for visitors.

Puto Tanay

A special type of steamed rice cake, Puto Tanay is soft and slightly sweet, often served as a snack or dessert. It is commonly made with coconut milk, giving it a rich and smooth flavor that is unique to the area.
