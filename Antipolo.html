Antipolo City

Pilgrimage Capital of the Philippines

Tradition

Antipolo, a city in Rizal Province, is known for its rich cultural heritage, one of which is the tradition of Alay Lakad (or "Offerings Walk"). This tradition is closely tied to the city's religious practices, particularly the devotion to Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage (Nuestra Señora de la Paz y Buen Viaje), the patroness of Antipolo.


The Alay Lakad is an annual pilgrimage where thousands of devotees walk from nearby towns or even from Metro Manila to the Antipolo Cathedral, offering their prayers and expressing their gratitude for the Virgin Mary's guidance and protection. The event typically takes place during the May pilgrimage season, culminating in the feast day of the Virgin on May 31st.


The walk itself can span several kilometers, with participants often starting early in the morning and walking for hours. It's both a spiritual and physical journey, symbolizing the devotion and faith of the people. For many, it is not only an act of religious devotion but also a means of thanksgiving for the blessings they've received. Some people even walk barefoot, a symbol of humility and complete submission to God's will.


The Alay Lakad has become an important cultural and religious event in Antipolo, reflecting the deep sense of faith and community that binds its people. It's a time for families and friends to come together in prayer, and for newcomers to experience the city's devotion firsthand. The tradition remains a testament to Antipolo's vibrant spiritual heritage and its lasting connection to the Filipino people's devotion to the Virgin Mary.

Festivals

SuMaKaH Festival

Celebrated every April, this festival showcases Antipolo’s local products: Suman, Mangga, Kasoy, and Hamaka. It features street dancing, food fairs, and exhibits that honor the city's rich traditions.

Maytime Festival

Held in May, this is the most well-known celebration tied to the devotion to Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage. It includes processions, pilgrimages, and the traditional Alay Lakad, drawing thousands of devotees.

Tipulo Festival

Held every May 1, this grand fiesta celebrates Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage, with mass and processions.

Must Visit

Antipolo Cathedral

This historic church is the heart of Antipolo’s religious life. Pilgrims visit to pray and pay homage to the miraculous image of the Virgin Mary. It’s especially popular during May for the Alay Lakad and other religious events.

Hinulugang Taktak

A famous natural attraction and historical waterfall, Hinulugang Taktak is a great spot for nature lovers. The area has been developed into a park with picnic areas, pools, and hanging bridges—perfect for a relaxing day trip.

Pinto Art Museum

A unique museum that features contemporary Filipino art, set in a beautiful garden and Mediterranean-inspired villa. It's a favorite for art lovers, photographers, and tourists looking for something visually and culturally enriching.

Local Foods

Suman sa Ibos

A sticky rice delicacy wrapped in coconut leaves, often served with sugar or ripe mangoes. It’s one of Antipolo’s most iconic pasalubong (souvenir treats).

Kasoy (Cashew Nuts)

Antipolo is known for its freshly roasted or sugar-coated cashew nuts, a favorite snack and gift item for visitors.

Manggang Hilaw with Bagoong

Green mangoes served with shrimp paste—a sour and salty street food favorite that’s a staple in Antipolo, especially during fiestas and pilgrimages.
