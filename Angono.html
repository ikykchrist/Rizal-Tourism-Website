Angono

Art Capital of the Philippines

Tradition

Angono, a vibrant town in Rizal Province, is widely known as the “Art Capital of the Philippines” because of its deep connection to visual arts, music, and folk traditions. Its most iconic cultural celebration is the Higantes Festival, held every November in honor of San Clemente, the patron saint of fishermen. This tradition has become a symbol of Angono’s identity, blending religious devotion with creativity and artistry.


The highlight of the Higantes Festival is the grand parade of giant papier-mâché figures called Higantes, which are often as tall as 12 feet. These towering figures were originally created as a form of protest during the Spanish colonial period, mocking the oppressive landlords. Today, they are beautifully handcrafted by local artists and represent different characters, professions, and social roles, making each one unique and colorful.


Aside from the Higantes, the festival features vibrant street dancing, cultural performances, and the traditional basaan, where people splash water on each other as a symbol of blessing and renewal. The streets of Angono come alive with music, food stalls, and festive decorations, drawing both locals and tourists to celebrate the town’s spirit.


Angono's tradition is also deeply tied to its legacy in the arts. It is the hometown of two National Artists — Carlos “Botong” Francisco for Visual Arts and Lucio San Pedro for Music — whose works continue to inspire the town’s cultural identity. Art galleries, murals, and workshops are spread throughout the town, making art a living part of everyday life in Angono.


The traditions of Angono are a beautiful blend of faith, history, and artistic expression. Through its festivals and cultural practices, the town continues to honor its past while inspiring future generations to celebrate their creativity and heritage.

Festivals

Higantes Festival

The most famous festival in Angono, held every November, in honor of San Clemente, the patron saint of fishermen. It features giant papier-mâché puppets (higantes), colorful parades, street dancing, and the traditional water dousing or basaan.

Angono AT Fest (Art and Tourism Festival)

Celebrated in October, this festival highlights Angono’s identity as the “Art Capital of the Philippines.” It showcases local art, music, culinary events, and tourism-related activities to promote Angono's creative spirit.

Must Visit

Angono-Binangonan Petroglyphs

The oldest known artwork in the Philippines, this site features ancient rock carvings dating back to 3000 B.C. It’s a National Cultural Treasure and offers a fascinating glimpse into early Filipino civilization.

Blanco Family Museum

A museum showcasing the impressive works of the Blanco family, a clan of painters known for their detailed and realistic depictions of Filipino life and culture. Each generation of the family has contributed to this stunning collection.

Botong Francisco Museum

The ancestral home and museum of Carlos “Botong” Francisco, National Artist for Visual Arts. It features his personal items, sketches, and paintings, honoring his legacy and influence on Philippine mural art.

Local Foods

Paksiw na Ayungin

A traditional fish dish made with ayungin (silver perch), a freshwater fish commonly caught in Laguna de Bay. It’s cooked in vinegar, garlic, and pepper, often served during fiestas and family gatherings.

Suman sa Lihiya

A type of sticky rice delicacy wrapped in banana leaves and cooked with lye water (lihiya), giving it a distinct flavor and soft texture. It’s usually paired with sweet coconut caramel sauce or sugar.

Minanok na Itik

A native duck stew cooked in ginger and lemongrass, showcasing the town’s rural culinary roots. It's a flavorful dish often prepared during special occasions or shared among families.
