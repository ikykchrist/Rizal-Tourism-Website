Binangonan

The place where someting or someone has voluntarily risen

Tradition

Binangonan, a lakeside town in Rizal Province, is rich in religious and cultural traditions that reflect its deep historical roots and strong community ties.
One of the most cherished traditions in Binangonan is the Pagoda Festival in honor of Sta. Ursula, the town's patron saint.
Celebrated every October 21, this tradition features a grand fluvial parade where a decorated pagoda carrying the image of the saint sails along the shores of Laguna de Bay.
Accompanied by music, dancing, and prayers, the event is a vibrant expression of faith and devotion among the locals.

Another tradition is the Panata ng mga Deboto, where devotees walk barefoot or carry religious images as part of their spiritual promise or thanksgiving.
This practice reflects the people’s deep Catholic faith and their gratitude for blessings received.
It is most visible during Holy Week and local fiestas.

Binangonan also observes Holy Week with solemnity and reverence.
The townspeople participate in processions and the Senakulo, a theatrical reenactment of the Passion of Christ, staged in public areas.
These religious plays are passed down through generations and serve as both devotion and a cultural heritage.

Additionally, traditional fishing practices remain alive among residents living near Laguna de Bay.
Fishing not only sustains local livelihoods but also keeps ancestral knowledge and skills alive.
The lake culture, combined with religious rituals and festivals, shapes the identity of Binangonan as a town that deeply values both its faith and its way of life.

Festivals

Pagoda Festival (Feast of Sta. Ursula)

Celebrated every October 21, the Pagoda Festival is the most iconic event in Binangonan. It features a fluvial parade along Laguna de Bay in honor of Sta. Ursula, the town's patron saint. The highlight is a pagoda (decorated boat) carrying the saint’s image, accompanied by music, prayers, and festive activities. Locals and visitors alike join in devotion and celebration.

Binalayan Festival

The Binalayan Festival is celebrated every November in Barangay Binitagan, Binangonan. It honors the community’s fishing and farming traditions through street dancing, parades, and cultural performances. The festival brings locals together to celebrate their heritage and the hardworking spirit of their town.

Must Visit

Talim Island & Mount Tagapo

Talim Island, situated in the heart of Laguna de Bay, offers a tranquil escape from urban life. Its highlight is Mount Tagapo, also known as Bundok Susong Dalaga due to its conical shape. Standing at 438 meters, this peak provides panoramic views of the lake, Metro Manila skyline, and surrounding mountains. The hike is beginner-friendly, starting from Barangay Janosa in Binangonan.

Santa Ursula Parish Church

A historical and religious landmark, the Santa Ursula Parish Church was built during the Spanish colonial period and remains a centerpiece of local faith. Its old stone structure and peaceful surroundings attract both pilgrims and tourists, especially during the Pagoda Festival every October in honor of Sta. Ursula.

Eastridge View Deck

Located within the Eastridge area, this view deck offers sweeping panoramic views of Metro Manila, Laguna de Bay, and even parts of Rizal’s mountain ranges. It's a popular stop for quick sightseeing, photos, and appreciating the natural beauty of the area. There are also cafes and golf courses nearby, making it a chill spot to relax after a day of exploring.

Local Foods

Inihaw na Tilapia at Bangus

Because Binangonan is located along the shores of Laguna de Bay, fresh fish like tilapia and bangus (milkfish) are abundant. Locals often enjoy them grilled (inihaw), seasoned simply with salt, and served with soy sauce, calamansi, and chili. It’s a staple in many homes and eateries near the lake.

Pritong Itik (Fried Duck)

A savory specialty in Binangonan and nearby areas, pritong itik is deep-fried native duck that’s crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside. It’s commonly sold in roadside stalls and best paired with rice and vinegar dipping sauce.

Minanok na Baka

This lesser-known Rizal dish, also found in parts of Binangonan, is like a local beef stew. Despite the name "minanok" (which means "cooked like chicken"), it uses beef but is stewed in a ginger broth with leafy vegetables and root crops — similar in comfort to a sinigang or nilaga.
