Montalban

Where Culture and Adventure Come Together

Tradition

Montalban, also known as Rodriguez, is a town in Rizal known for its strong Catholic faith and cultural heritage.
One of the most significant traditions is the Feast of St. Jerome, the town's patron saint, celebrated every September 30.
The feast includes processions, novenas, and a grand parade, with locals coming together for religious devotion and celebration.

Another important tradition is the Pasko sa Montalban during Christmas.
This event showcases a variety of parades, Christmas lights displays, and community activities, reflecting the town's vibrant holiday spirit.

Montalban also has a deep connection with its natural surroundings, particularly the Wawa Dam.
Locals celebrate the area’s rich history and contributions to the community with various cultural events throughout the year.

Festivals

Feast of St. Jerome

The Feast of St. Jerome, celebrated every September 30, is the town’s most important religious event. It honors the patron saint of Montalban with a grand parade, religious processions, and novenas, bringing the community together for devotion and celebration.

Pasko sa Montalban

Celebrated during the Christmas season, Pasko sa Montalban is a festive event marked by parades, Christmas lights displays, and various community activities. It reflects the town's joy and spirit during the holiday season, with locals joining in the merriment and celebrating the Christmas spirit.

Wawa Festival

The Wawa Festival is an annual event that celebrates the town's heritage tied to the Wawa Dam. It includes cultural presentations, fun activities, and fishing contests, honoring the natural beauty and history of the area while promoting community involvement.

Must Visit

Wawa Dam

A popular destination for nature lovers, Wawa Dam offers a refreshing escape with its flowing river, scenic rock formations, and bamboo rafts. It's ideal for picnics, trekking, and swimming, surrounded by the lush Sierra Madre mountains.

Avilon Zoo

The Avilon Zoo is one of the largest zoos in the Philippines. It’s home to a wide variety of animals and offers educational tours, making it a favorite spot for families, students, and animal lovers.

Mt. Pamitinan

For adventure seekers, Mt. Pamitinan offers a thrilling hike with limestone cliffs, panoramic views, and historical significance. It’s believed to be where the Katipuneros once gathered during the revolution against Spain.

Local Foods

Inihaw na Tilapia at Hito

Thanks to the nearby rivers and dam, Montalban offers fresh and flavorful grilled tilapia and hito (catfish). These are often served with spicy vinegar and eaten with rice.

Sinigang na Baboy sa Bayabas

A twist on the classic sinigang, this version uses guava (bayabas) for a subtly sweet and tangy flavor. It's a comforting and well-loved dish in the area.

Pansit Miki

Locally made miki noodles are often used in hearty pancit dishes in Montalban, usually stir-fried with vegetables, pork, and sometimes quail eggs. It’s a popular handa during town fiestas and gatherings.
